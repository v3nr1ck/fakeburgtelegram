**MECHANICSBURG** — Village council spent 47 minutes Monday night debating whether a fourth stop sign on Main Street would “restore order” or “start a civil war between the church parking lot and the Dollar General.”

Council ultimately tabled the measure 4–2, directing the street committee to study traffic counts “when someone remembers who has the counter.”

Mayor Dale Hopson said the issue had “united the village in disagreement.” Residents who spoke during public comment included two people who wanted the sign, one who wanted a roundabout, and one who asked if the meeting could end before the high school baseball game.

“I’ve lived here 40 years,” said resident Patty Kern. “We used to wave. Now we brake-check each other like we’re on I-70.”

Public Works said paint for new crosswalks remains on backorder, and that the “temporary cones” near the post office have been temporary since March 2024.

A final vote is expected after the Champaign County Fair, or after the next tractor parade, whichever comes first.
